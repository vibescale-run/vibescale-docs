## Grade and Score

The report header shows:

- **Letter grade** — from **A** (best) to **E** (critical), with label in English:

| Grade | Label |
|-------|-------|
| A | Excellent |
| B | Good |
| C | Attention Needed |
| D | Poor |
| E | Critical |

- **Numeric score** — from 0 to 100.
- **Summary** — a sentence reflecting the overall state, such as:
  - *"X findings in your code need attention"*
  - *"Only inherited dependencies — no urgent actions"*
  - *"No issues detected"*

Click the **methodology** link next to the summary to see exactly how the score is calculated.

## Finding Categories

Each finding belongs to one of the three categories below. Understanding the difference is the most important step to prioritize correctly.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Authored — issues in your code">
    Vulnerabilities introduced directly by the code your team wrote. **Prioritize these.** They carry maximum weight in the score. If the Authored count is greater than zero, the score will be significantly affected.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Inherited — vulnerabilities from libraries">
    Flaws in third-party packages your project imports. They deserve review — especially critical and high — but fixing them depends on updating the package version, not changing your code directly.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Noise — development tools">
    Findings from packages used only in development that never reach production. **Do not affect your score.** They are hidden by default.

    To view them, click **"Show X noise items (dev-only)"** at the bottom of the list.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Severity Levels

Within each category, findings are ordered by severity:

| Severity | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **Critical** | Immediate risk of compromise. Fix before anything else. |
| **High** | Serious risk that should be resolved in the next sprint. |
| **Medium** | Moderate risk. Plan the fix. |
| **Low** | Limited impact. Fix when convenient. |

## EPSS Badges

Some findings display an additional badge indicating real exploitation risk:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="🔴 Active Exploitation" icon="fire">
    **≥ 90th percentile of EPSS.** This finding is among the most likely to be exploited in the next 30 days, according to FIRST.org data. Prioritize fixing immediately.
  </Card>
  <Card title="🟠 Elevated Risk" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    **Between the 50th and 90th percentile.** Above industry median. Worth scheduling a fix.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  EPSS measures the **real probability of exploitation**, not just technical severity. A Medium severity finding with an **Active Exploitation** badge may be more urgent than a High without a badge.
</Note>

## Finding Details

Click any finding to see:

- Title and severity badge (e.g., CRITICAL RISK, HIGH RISK)
- Problem description
- Affected location — file path or module name
- Code snippet — when available
- Fix suggestion
- Classification — Authored / Inherited / Noise

> **Important:** The fix suggestion is only shown if considered useful and does not duplicate the finding’s title or description. This avoids repeated information and improves report clarity.

## Available Actions

### Mark as Resolved

Click the checkbox next to the finding to mark it as resolved after applying the fix.

### Create a Kanban Task

Click **Generate task(s)** to send the finding directly to your Kanban board. Priority is mapped automatically:

| Severity | Kanban Priority |
|----------|-----------------|
| Critical | P1 |
| High | P2 |
| Medium | P3 |

After creating the task, a status badge appears on the finding: **To Do / In Progress / In Review / Done**.

### Use AI Fix Prompt

Click **Copy prompt** to copy a remediation prompt ready to paste into your preferred code assistant — Copilot, Cursor, Bolt, or similar. The prompt includes the finding’s context and fix instructions.

> **Note:** The fix prompt is only shown when the remediation suggestion is considered useful and not repetitive compared to the finding’s title or description.

## Unlocking the Re-Check

The **Re-Check** button remains locked until **all Critical and High findings** are marked as resolved or dismissed.

<Warning>
  Re-Check will not be enabled while any Critical or High findings remain open — regardless of category. Resolve or dismiss each to unlock the option.
</Warning>

The Re-Check button now shows a visual and status indicator when running. While Re-Check is in progress, the button displays a spinner and the text "Starting Re-Check..." and is disabled to prevent multiple simultaneous runs.
