Alignment is the step where you enter the conversation. The platform makes inferences, but you know your app better than anyone — and the final report is built from your confirmed answers, not from automated assumptions.

This step exists to ensure the opportunity analysis reflects the real intent of the product, not just what the code suggests from a distance.

## Why confirm the data?

Discovery extracts information directly from the repository, but code and product intent don't always align perfectly. A repository may have experimental features that don't represent the product's core, or name things technically in ways that don't translate well to market language.

<Note>
  The Opportunity Report is generated exclusively from the data you confirm in this step. No unconfirmed inference is used in the final report.
</Note>

## What you review

A form presents the fields for confirmation. The fields reviewed in this step are:

- **Problem solved** — the main pain point the app addresses
- **Target audience** — who the product was built for
- **Differentiator** — what makes your app distinct from alternatives
- **Wow Moment** — the moment the product's value becomes evident to the user
- **Use case** — the most important concrete usage scenario
- **Alternative solution** — what users would do without your app
- **Core feature** — the central feature that sustains the value proposition
- **Competitors** — products competing in the same market space

## How to confirm or edit a field

<Steps>
  <Step title="Read the inferred value">
    For each field, the platform displays the value it automatically identified during Discovery. Read it carefully before confirming.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm or edit">
    If the value is correct, click **Confirm** to proceed. If it's wrong or incomplete, edit the field directly in the form and then confirm.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Move to the next field">
    Click **Next** to go to the following field. Repeat until you've reviewed all fields.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete alignment">
    After the last field, Alignment is marked as **Completed** and report generation starts automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tips for more useful answers

<Tip>
  Write as you would describe your product to an investor in 30 seconds — clear, direct, and jargon-free. The report uses these answers to assess market potential, so the more precise you are, the more useful the analysis will be.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  Don't skip fields or leave generic values just to move faster. Vague answers produce vague analyses. Spend 5 minutes here to receive a report worth reading.
</Warning>
